# attas

**attas** stands for **Autonomous Trading and Treasury Agents System**.

attas is a collaborative, AI-native network for financial intelligence. It is designed to help the financial industry move from disconnected tools and siloed workflows to a system where data vendors, service providers, analysts, traders, investors, and institutions can contribute through their own agents and work together in one intelligent ecosystem.

In the AI era, finance does not only need better models. It needs a better way to connect data, analytics, expert judgment, research, and client context. attas is built for that shift.

## Why attas

Finance already has intelligence everywhere, but that intelligence is fragmented.

- Data lives with vendors and internal systems.
- Analytics live with service providers and internal models.
- Research lives in reports, dashboards, terminals, and spreadsheets.
- Expert judgment lives inside analysts, traders, and relationship managers.
- Client context lives inside financial institutions.

Most financial workflows still depend on manually stitching these pieces together. attas is designed to turn those isolated pieces into a collaborative network for financial intelligence.

## Core idea

attas is built on a simple idea:

**Every participant in finance can own an agent, contribute value through that agent, and become part of a larger intelligence network.**

That means:

- A financial data vendor can provide and explain its own data through an agent.
- A service provider can expose analytics, models, or workflow capabilities through an agent.
- An analyst can contribute structured insight and market interpretation through an agent.
- A trader or investor can turn a research method into a reusable workflow.
- A financial institution can assemble all of these inputs into highly personalized intelligence for internal teams or end clients.

## Architecture

The attas ecosystem is built around a collaborative agent network.

### Plaza
**Directory and coordination layer**

Plaza is the discovery and relay layer of the network. Agents connect to at least one Plaza to register their capabilities, discover other agents, and communicate across the ecosystem.

### Pulser
**Data and signal agents**

Pulser agents provide access to structured financial resources such as schemas, field definitions, real-time data, historical data, signals, analytics outputs, or research artifacts. Data vendors, service providers, analysts, and internal teams can all deploy Pulsers.

### Phemar
**Structured intelligence agents**

Phemar agents combine data, context, explanation, and interpretation into structured financial intelligence. They help transform fragmented inputs into usable understanding for downstream workflows.

### Castr
**Reporting and delivery agents**

Castr agents generate the final output for specific audiences. Outputs may include briefings, investment research, treasury summaries, portfolio commentary, recommendations, or client-facing reports.

## What attas helps the financial industry do

### 1. Connect isolated financial services into one collaborative system
attas helps turn disconnected data sources, analytics services, research workflows, and reporting pipelines into one intelligent network. Instead of treating each participant as a separate tool, attas allows each one to contribute through its own agent.

### 2. Let vendors and providers bring expertise with their products
Financial data and analytics are only useful when users understand their meaning, assumptions, and limitations. attas allows vendors and service providers to expose not only access, but also guidance, schema explanation, usage patterns, and best practices through their own agents.

### 3. Enable analysts, traders, and investors to contribute directly
Some of the most valuable insight in finance comes from specialized experts and independent workflows. attas gives analysts, traders, and investors a way to turn that knowledge into reusable services and structured intelligence.

### 4. Help institutions build personalized intelligence
Banks, asset managers, treasury teams, and wealth platforms can combine vendor data, third-party analytics, analyst views, internal models, and client context into outputs tailored to specific users, portfolios, mandates, and communication styles.

### 5. Make cost visible before a workflow runs
In attas, services can be priced by their owners. Users can see expected costs before consuming data, analytics, premium insight, or report generation.

### 6. Use economics to improve workflows
Cost is not only a billing mechanism. It is also an economic signal that helps optimize workflow design. When each meaningful step has a visible cost, users and institutions can choose better paths based on value, quality, latency, and efficiency.

### 7. Reward contribution across the ecosystem
attas supports a model where value flows back to contributors. Data providers, analytics firms, analysts, and report creators can define price tags for their services and be rewarded when those services are used.

## Economic model

attas is designed as both a collaboration network and a service economy.

- Each agent owner can define pricing for the services they provide.
- Users know the cost before they use a service.
- Cost visibility helps institutions optimize workflows economically, not just technically.
- Contributors can be rewarded directly for the value they provide.

This makes attas suitable for a future in which financial intelligence is assembled from multiple providers, each contributing specialized value.
